About:

This website is our platform to share what we're learning on the our Journey in IamtheCODE. As four Grade 12 students, we're diving into the world of technology, gaining hands-on training, mentorship, and access to a global network. We're being empowered to code, create, and change the world, and we're excited to share our experiences, building confidence, encouraging new ideas, and inspiring a love for computer programming along the way.


What is iamtheCODE?



IamtheCODE is a global initiative aiming to empower 1 million young women and girls to code by 2030. They provide tech and digital skills training through programs like hackathons, digital clubs, and mentorships. Partnering with organizations like Coursera and Skillsoft, they focus on achieving the UN's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Operating through four pillars – People, Programs, Processes, and Platform – IamtheCODE has reached over 550,000 women and girls in 88 countries and is celebrating its 10th anniversary in 2025.

ENCOUNTER

JavaScript

The Action JavaScript adds movement and interaction to your website. It’s like the brain that makes things happen when someone clicks or types.

•Make buttons work and update parts of the page

•Use events to respond to user actions

•Work with Promises to handle information (like loading data)

•Try tools that help keep your code neat and working

•Understand how to debug (fix) your code when something goes wrong JavaScript makes your website smart and interactive.

Python

The Power Behind the Scenes Python is used to build the parts of websites and apps that users don’t see—but that make everything work.

•Organize and store information

•Solve problems using logic and code

•Build websites that can talk to users and databases

•Analyze data and even create basic AI tools

•Use Python to build smart, helpful programs Python helps you build powerful tools and systems.

CSS

The Style CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) makes your website look good. It’s like the clothes and colors you put on your HTML.

•Choose colors, fonts, and layouts

•Make your site look great on phones and computers

•Arrange things on the page with boxes and spacing

•Fix problems when different browsers show things differently

•Keep your styles organized and easy to update

HTML

The Structure HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is where everything begins. It’s like the skeleton of a website.

•Use tags to add text, images, links, and videos

•Create headings, paragraphs, and lists

•Build forms so people can type in their info

•Make your site accessible for everyone, including people with disabilities

•Keep your code clean and correct so it works well
