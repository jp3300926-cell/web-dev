About:

This website is our platform to share what we're learning on the our Journey in IamtheCODE. As four Grade 12 students, we're diving into the world of technology, gaining hands-on training, mentorship, and access to a global network. We're being empowered to code, create, and change the world, and we're excited to share our experiences, building confidence, encouraging new ideas, and inspiring a love for computer programming along the way.


What is iamtheCODE?



IamtheCODE is a global initiative aiming to empower 1 million young women and girls to code by 2030. They provide tech and digital skills training through programs like hackathons, digital clubs, and mentorships. Partnering with organizations like Coursera and Skillsoft, they focus on achieving the UN's Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Operating through four pillars – People, Programs, Processes, and Platform – IamtheCODE has reached over 550,000 women and girls in 88 countries and is celebrating its 10th anniversary in 2025.

TASK COORDINATION

Task coordination is just a fancy way of saying 'getting things done in a smart way.' When you're learning on Iamthecode, it's all about making sure you're making the most of your time and learning effectively. Here are some of the ways we approached our Iamthecode journey.

How to Coordinate Your Tasks on Iamthecode

Pick Your Learning Path:

•What it is: Learning paths are like guided tours through different topics.

•How to use it: Choose a learning path that matches what you want to learn. This helps you stay on track.

Set Mini-Goals

•What it is: Break down big courses into smaller, manageable chunks.

•How to use it: Decide what you want to achieve each day or week. This makes learning less overwhelming.

Use the "Assignments" Feature

•What it is: Some courses let you set assignments for yourself.

•How to use it: Create your own deadlines for finishing sections or courses. This keeps you accountable.

Join Groups

•What it is: Groups are like online study buddies.

•How to use it: Join groups related to your interests. Ask questions, share what you've learned, and get support from others.

Track Your Skills

•What it is: Iamthecode keeps track of the skills you're learning.

•How to use it: Check your skill progress to see how far you've come and what you need to work on.

Simple Steps to Stay Organized

- Spend a few minutes each week planning your learning schedule.

- Jot down key points as you go through courses.

- Go back over what you've learned to make sure it sticks.

- Try to learn a little bit every day or week.
